# Retrospective
---

**What Worked Well**
- We assigned roles and responsibilites in the beginning, ensuring everyone understood what they needed to do and that the workload was balanced.
- Our efficient communication allowed us to keep each other updated and answer any questions we had for one another quickly, allowing us to maintain a good workflow.

**Challenges We Faced**
- Due to the differences in our approaches to research & writing styles, maintaining consistency across the content for the topics we were contributing to was sometimes difficult.
- Meetings were not as frequest as we had initially hoped, due to our busy and conflicting schedules, however, we were still able to find some times where we were all available to meet and discuss the project.

**How Teamwork Could be Impoved**
- If we were to further divide each topic into sections and assign those sections to team members to research, it may have helped by giving the research of each topic a more equal and divided structure, rather than just adding to the research that the first contributer added
- If we were to agree on deadlines for each stage of the work, it may have resulted in a more organised, smoother workflow.


**What We Would Do Differently Next Time**
- Create the formatting template before starting the research and writing, rather than using it as a structure to follow while doing the reviews in order to improve consistency across the content for the topics.
- Set for each stage of the work to ensure consistent progression throughout the project and to allow sufficient time for contributions, formatting changes and reviews. 
- Assign specific sections of each topic to members to research in order to maintain a more structured approach and to minimise the risk of any duplicate information being added.
